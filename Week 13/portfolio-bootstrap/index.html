Introduction

Hello, my name is Ryan Jablonski. I am a student at Grand Valley State University. I am majoring in Information Systems (with a focus on software development) and minoring in Business.

Throughtout my time in college I have taken various computing courses where I've learned programming languages like Java and Python. I've also gained computing experience as a Systems Analyst intern at Koops Automation Systems where I used C# and platforms like Azure and Jira.

For as long as I can remember, I've always loved technology. Whether I was using our home computer, video game consoles, or the iPad's in my 3rd grade classroom, I always knew that I wanted to do something with technology when I grew up. The Information Systems program at GVSU gves me the opportunity to do that.

My interest in technology wasn't the only factor that swayed me towards majoring in Information Systems. I want to spend my professional life building computing solutions for our worlds biggest problems. To me, a fulfilling and successful career is one that is spent helping people. Computing technologies have already allowed us to do so much and I want to be a small part of the community that pushes the bounds of what we know.

During my free time, I participate in a life group through Campus Ministry, play various games like pool and ping-pong, and spend lots of time with friends.

For spring break this year, I went on a Campus Ministry mission trip to Nicaragua. While we were there, we lived with local families to learn about their culture and then headed to a childrens camp to destroy an old cabin and build new chairs.

My second highlight is that this semester was socially eventfull. I met a ton of new people and spent a lot of time with friends new and old.

Finally, I really enjoyed my website class. Web development is a topic that's always interested me so it was a lot of fun getting the chance to learn and use the technologies associated with web development.
